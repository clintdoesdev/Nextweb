FAQ

Common
Questions

What is Nextel Connect?
Nextel Connect is a regulated, NCC-compliant virtual telecom service provider. It gives you a real Nigerian phone number (eSIM) and rewards you with naira for every call you make, SMS you send, website visit you receive, and person you refer. Think of it as a telecom that puts its own revenue back into your pocket.
Is Nextel legit and legally registered?
Yes. Nextel is 100% real and legal. It is registered with the Corporate Affairs Commission (CAC) under BN No. 9628017 and licensed by the Nigerian Communications Commission (NCC) to operate as a virtual telecom provider. The certificate of registration was issued on June 22, 2026 by the Registrar-General.
What is the difference between Diamond and Royal eSIM?
Diamond (₦10,500) gives you local roaming, SMS rewards, WebGain, and referral bonuses — great for getting started. Royal (₦17,500) unlocks everything Diamond has, PLUS calls reward (₦288–₦628/min), higher SMS earnings, Global Roaming, 1% turnover commission, and maximum saved-contacts rewards. Royal is the highest-earning tier.
How and when can I withdraw my earnings?
Withdrawals are processed daily with no minimum threshold. You can withdraw via direct bank transfer to any Nigerian bank, load your Nextel V-Card (Verve) to spend globally, or convert your balance to airtime and data (VTU). Verified subscribers also qualify for a free Nextel POS terminal.
What is WebGain and how does it work?
Every Nextel subscriber gets a personal website page (nextal.com/YourName). You earn ₦7–₦20 (Diamond) or ₦15–₦50 (Royal) per visit to your page. By accepting Google Ads and matched brand ads, you earn additional income per view and per click — turning your Nextel page into a daily income-generating machine.
What rewards can I earn from referrals?
When someone you referred activates their eSIM, you earn a Connect Bonus of ₦300 (Diamond) or ₦600 (Royal) per referral. Beyond that, high-performing referrers unlock Nextel's Incentive & Achievement Rewards — including gadgets (iPhones, MacBooks, AirPods, smartwatches), branded merchandise, and international trips.
How does Nextel generate enough revenue to pay users?
Nextel earns from: eSIM sales and activations, sale of home fiber and fixed broadband lines, value-added subscription services (VAS), enterprise business solutions, network digital top-ups, and brand partnerships. These multiple revenue channels allow Nextel to distribute a portion of telecom earnings back to every subscriber sustainably.
Can I use my Nextel eSIM internationally?
Yes — but only on the Royal eSIM plan. Royal subscribers enjoy Global Roaming, meaning your Nextel number works internationally. Diamond subscribers are limited to Local Roaming (within Nigeria). Upgrade to Royal to unlock global connectivity and earning potential wherever you are.
